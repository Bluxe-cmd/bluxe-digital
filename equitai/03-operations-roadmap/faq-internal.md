## From FUNDERS AND INVESTORS

**Q: What makes EquitAI different from Code.org or Khan Academy?**

> EquitAI is not a curriculum company — it's a workforce operating system. Code.org and Khan Academy are K-12 awareness programs. They have no employer pipeline, no adult learner focus, no community governance, and no structural revenue beyond licensing and donations. EquitAI is designed to produce employment outcomes for high school seniors and adult career changers — and to fund itself through the data centers that need the talent we're training. It's a fundamentally different model.

---

**Q: Why Maryland? Why not go national?**

> We're starting in Maryland because Maryland is uniquely positioned right now: significant data center growth in PG and Charles Counties, an active Governor Moore administration with equity commitments, clear CBA leverage, and a community we know and are rooted in. Maryland-first means we can build real community trust, prove the model with real outcomes, and then license it nationally. National-first means spreading thin before we've proven anything.

---

**Q: How do you know CBAs will actually fund you?**

> Because data center developers need two things from CBAs that EquitAI uniquely delivers: (1) a workforce training partner who can actually execute — not just write a check; (2) a structured training-to-hire pipeline that gives their community relations team something real to point to. Without EquitAI (or an equivalent), they're stuck writing vague "community grants" that satisfy no one. We're solving their CBA problem, and they'll pay for it.

---

**Q: What if TEDCO doesn't fund you?**

> We have three other funding streams active simultaneously: Abell Foundation, Goldseker Foundation, and employer training contracts. TEDCO is the fastest path to $50K, but it's not our only path. If TEDCO says no, we have letters of interest from employers and foundation LOIs submitted that cover our Year 1 cash needs within a 60-day adjustment.

---

**Q: How do you prove impact?**

> Our primary impact metric is employment outcome — specifically, whether EquitAI graduates get AI-adjacent jobs and at what wage. We track: (1) completion rate; (2) 6-month employment rate; (3) average wage gain from pre-program baseline; (4) employer satisfaction. We share these quarterly with funders and annually with the public. No vanity metrics.

---

**Q: Is this a nonprofit or a business?**

> EquitAI operates as a hybrid: EquitAI, LLC for earned revenue (CBA contracts, employer fees) and EquitAI Education Fund (501(c)(3)) for grants and tax-deductible donations. The operational entity is structured for speed and contractual flexibility. The nonprofit arm is the fundraising vehicle. This is a deliberate choice — we don't want nonprofit governance overhead slowing down a contract we need to close in 72 hours.

## From EMPLOYERS

**Q: What credentials do your graduates have?**

> EquitAI graduates earn stackable certificates tied to hours of instruction and demonstrated skills: AI Foundations (40 hrs), Workforce Ready (80 hrs), and Technical Certificate (120 hrs). We're working with Prince George's Community College and College of Southern Maryland on credit articulation so graduates can also earn college credit. In the near term, our credentials are portfolio-backed — graduates present a project demonstrating applied AI skills.

---

**Q: How do I know EquitAI graduates are actually ready to hire?**

> We'll give you a portfolio and a skills summary for every graduate, plus we'll facilitate a pre-interview skills demonstration. If you hire through us and the hire doesn't work out within 90 days, we'll provide a replacement candidate at no additional cost. We're building our reputation on employer satisfaction — we have no incentive to send you unprepared candidates.

---

**Q: What does it cost?**

> If you're participating as a pipeline employer (interview commitment only), there's no cost. You get access to our talent pool and we make intros. If you want to fund a dedicated cohort for your specific workforce needs, typical training investment is $1,800–$3,200 per employee depending on the track. That investment may qualify for Maryland workforce training tax credits — reducing your net cost by 50–100%.

---

**Q: What AI roles do your graduates qualify for?**

> After Track A (40 hrs): AI-aware operational roles — customer experience, administrative AI tools, data entry with AI assist. After Track B (80 hrs): AI support roles — operations analyst, AI tool implementation, data analytics support. After Track D (120 hrs): Near-hire technical roles — junior data analyst, AI implementation support, data center operations technician.

## From DATA CENTER DEVELOPERS

**Q: Do we have a legal obligation to have a CBA?**

> In Maryland, CBA requirements vary by county and project type. Prince George's County has active CBA discussions for major development projects. Charles County is earlier stage. We're not here to tell you what your legal obligations are — we're here to help you meet them (and exceed them) in a way that creates real value for your community and real talent for your operation.

---

**Q: We already have a community relations team. Why do we need EquitAI?**

> Your community relations team is excellent at relationships. EquitAI is the implementation partner that turns your CBA commitment into actual workers trained and actual hires made. We're not competing with your community relations team — we're the operational mechanism they need to make workforce commitments real. Think of us as your CBA execution arm.

---

**Q: What if we just write a check to a local nonprofit?**

> You can. But a check to a general nonprofit with no workforce focus doesn't produce AI-trained workers for your facility. EquitAI produces credentialed, employer-ready candidates in the specific skills your operation needs. If you're going to invest in community workforce training, invest in something that gives your hiring team something to work with.

## From HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS + PARENTS

**Q: Will colleges accept EquitAI credits?**

> We're working on credit articulation with Prince George's Community College and College of Southern Maryland — meaning EquitAI credentials could count toward their degree programs. We'll have that formalized in Year 2. In the meantime, EquitAI credentials are recognized by our employer partners and documented in student portfolios.

---

**Q: Does it cost anything?**

> The program is free to students. It's funded through employer and data center partnerships. We may offer a small stipend ($50–$100) to help with transportation costs for students who complete the program.

---

**Q: What kind of jobs can I get after EquitAI?**

> EquitAI graduates from Track D (120 hours) are eligible for interviews at our employer partners, with starting wages of $18–$25/hour. That's significantly above minimum wage and above many jobs you'd get straight out of high school. The goal is to get you into a career, not just a job — and AI skills are transferable to almost every field.

---

## From COMMUNITY ORGANIZATIONS

**Q: How do we know EquitAI won't just extract from our community?**

> EquitAI's governance structure is specifically designed to prevent extraction. Local Advisory Councils — made up of community members, parents, and graduates — have real review power over our curriculum, our outcomes data, and our priorities. Our facilitators are from the community. Our partnerships are with local institutions. And our funding model (CBAs) requires that data center developers invest in community benefit — EquitAI channels that investment back to residents rather than allowing it to flow to outside organizations.

---

**Q: What do you need from us?**

> In a partnership: participant referrals, space access, and trust. We bring the curriculum, the facilitators, the employer connections, and the funding. You bring the community connection we can't manufacture. It's a genuine partnership — not a vendor relationship.
