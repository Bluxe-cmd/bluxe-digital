**Purpose:** Quick reference for common strategic decisions  
**When to use:** When you're at a fork in the road and need a framework, not a conversation

## DECISION TREE 1: Which Funding Source to Pursue First?

```
START: Do you have a Maryland LLC or nonprofit registered?
├── NO → Register LLC first (businessexpress.maryland.gov | 1-2 weeks)
│         └── THEN return to this tree
└── YES → Continue ↓

Is the TEDCO pre-seed application window open?
├── YES → Apply immediately (highest non-dilutive amount, Maryland-specific)
│          └── THEN pursue all other sources in parallel
└── NO → Note the next deadline and set a calendar reminder
           └── Continue ↓

Do you have a potential employer willing to pay for AI training?
├── YES → Draft employer training agreement FIRST (fastest cash)
│          └── THEN apply for grants
└── NO → Start employer outreach this week (see email sequences)
           └── Continue ↓

Is there an active data center project with CBA negotiations underway in MD?
├── YES → Engage CBA process immediately (highest contract value)
│          └── ALSO apply for grants in parallel
└── NO → Monitor + research (see data center CBA playbook)
           └── Apply for grants: TEDCO → Abell → Goldseker → VLT

YEAR 2 CHECK:
Did Year 1 grants + employer fees cover operations?
├── YES → Invest in SBIR Phase I application for Year 2-3 platform funding
└── NO → Deepen CBA pipeline before applying for SBIR
```

## DECISION TREE 2: Should EquitAI Form a Nonprofit?

```
Do you need to apply for a grant that requires 501(c)(3) status?
├── NO → Stay as LLC for now; revisit in 6 months
└── YES → Is there an available fiscal sponsor?
           ├── YES → Use fiscal sponsor for this grant (faster)
           │          └── File IRS Form 1023 in parallel for future
           └── NO → File IRS Form 1023-EZ ASAP (fastest nonprofit path)

What is your primary Year 1 revenue mix?
├── Mostly CBA + employer contracts (>60%) → Keep LLC; add nonprofit when grants become significant
├── Mostly grants (>60%) → Form nonprofit first; LLC can wait
└── Balanced → Form both; LLC for operations, nonprofit for fundraising
```

## DECISION TREE 3: Which Space to Use for a Cohort?

```
START: Do you have a current cohort ready to launch (participants enrolled)?
├── NO → Use library or community partner space (no cost while validating)
└── YES → How many participants?
           ├── Under 15 → Library meeting room (book now; free)
           ├── 15–25 → Library computer lab OR community college classroom
           └── 25+ → Negotiate community college space or larger partner site

Is the cohort funded by a CBA or employer contract?
├── YES → Consider negotiating employer/developer site delivery
│          └── OR use community site and include space costs in contract budget
└── NO → Always use free/low-cost space (library, faith community, partner org)

Is the cohort an evening cohort (after 5pm)?
├── YES → Confirm: Does the space have evening access?
│          ├── YES → Proceed
│          └── NO → Look for alternative site with evening hours
└── NO (daytime) → Most spaces work; library is ideal
```

## DECISION TREE 4: Is This CBA Opportunity Worth Pursuing?

```
Is there an active data center project with community review pending?
├── NO → Not yet; keep monitoring (see CBA playbook for monitoring steps)
└── YES → Continue ↓

What is the estimated project size (investment value)?
├── Under $50M → Small project; CBA training obligation may be < $50K
│               → Pursue only if no larger opportunities exist
├── $50M–$250M → Medium project; CBA training obligation likely $50K–$200K
│               → Worth pursuing
└── Over $250M → Large project; CBA training obligation could be $200K+
                → PRIORITY — engage immediately

Is there already a community coalition involved?
├── YES → Contact coalition lead immediately
│          → Propose EquitAI as the workforce training partner
└── NO → Do you have time to build a coalition?
           ├── YES → Identify 2-3 community organizations + begin coalition building
           └── NO (too early) → Make introductory contact with developer directly
                                  + add to watchlist for coalition development

Has the developer signaled openness to a CBA?
├── YES → Move to Stage 3 of CBA Negotiation Playbook
├── UNKNOWN → Send introductory email first (see email sequences)
└── NO (developer resistant) → Escalate through community coalition
                                 + county council engagement
```

## DECISION TREE 5: Should We Add This Partner?

```
Does the partner share EquitAI's target population (HS students + adult career changers)?
├── NO → Probably not the right partner
└── YES → Continue ↓

What does the partner bring?
├── Space → Score: +3 (valuable in Year 1)
├── Participants/referrals → Score: +4 (most valuable in Year 1)
├── Funding access → Score: +5 (critical in Year 1)
├── Government relationships → Score: +4
├── Credibility/trust → Score: +3
└── Technical expertise → Score: +2

What does EquitAI give up?
├── Revenue share → Only if absolutely necessary; keep low (< 15%)
├── Decision-making control → Caution: Don't give partners veto power
├── Branding → EquitAI brand must remain primary
└── Time → Every partnership takes management time; be selective

Is the partner relationship transactional or genuine?
├── Transactional (they want something specific from you) → Proceed cautiously; formalize terms
└── Genuine (they believe in the mission) → Strong partnership potential
```

## DECISION TREE 6: How to Respond to a Cold Opportunity (Someone Calls You)

```
Someone contacts EquitAI with a potential opportunity. Before responding:

Step 1: What is the opportunity type?
├── Funding (grant, contract, investment) → "Yes, let's talk" — respond within 24 hours
├── Partnership (school, employer, nonprofit) → "Yes, let's explore" — respond within 48 hours
├── Speaking/press → "Yes, happy to" — respond within 24 hours
└── Something else → Evaluate: Does this advance any of the 5 revenue streams?
                       ├── YES → Respond within 48 hours
                       └── NO → Politely decline or defer

Step 2: What do they actually want?
├── Do they want something FROM EquitAI (money, content, free services)? → Caution
├── Do they want to GIVE something to EquitAI (space, referrals, funding)? → Priority
└── Do they want mutual benefit? → Ideal — explore further

Step 3: Is this worth a meeting?
├── Does the opportunity connect to Year 1 revenue targets? → Yes = worth 30 minutes
├── Is the person/org credible in their field? → Check LinkedIn, Google, references
└── Does it distract from Week 1 priorities? → If yes, defer to Month 2 calendar

RULE: Don't say yes to every meeting. Say yes to meetings that advance
your 3 current priorities. Defer everything else.
```

## DECISION TREE 7: Employer Partner — Prospect Qualification

```
Is the employer based in Maryland or do they hire Maryland workers?
├── NO → Not a CBA or government contract opportunity; skip
└── YES → Continue ↓

Do they have AI-adjacent roles (data, tech, operations, cybersecurity)?
├── NO → Not a near-term pipeline fit
│        → Could still be an employer training (AI productivity) customer
└── YES → Continue ↓

Are they hiring now or in the next 12 months?
├── NO → Add to long-term list; revisit in 6 months
└── YES → Priority prospect — advance to email sequence

Do they have Maryland CBA obligations or workforce training tax credit eligibility?
├── YES → Lead with this in outreach (reduces their net cost)
└── NO → Lead with talent pipeline + ROI framing

Company size?
├── < 50 employees → Individual training contract (Track 2A, small cohort)
├── 50–500 employees → Employer training contract (custom cohort; $20K–$80K)
└── 500+ employees → Strategic partner tier (multi-year; could include CBA negotiation support)
```
