## SBIR Phase I Application Outline (NSF)

### Section 1: Significance (1 page)
> The AI workforce gap in Maryland's data center growth corridors represents a structural market failure. Over $4 billion in data center investment has been approved in Maryland since 2020, primarily in Prince George's and Charles Counties. These facilities create an estimated [X] AI-adjacent jobs, but fewer than [X]% of nearby residents hold qualifying credentials. Current AI literacy solutions are either K-12 (not workforce-ready) or enterprise (not accessible to low-income learners). EquitAI addresses this gap with a community-governed AI literacy operating system that delivers stackable credentials to high school seniors and adult learners, connected directly to employer apprenticeship pipelines.

### Section 2: Innovation (1 page)
> EquitAI's innovation is the **CBA-integrated AI literacy OS model**: a technology platform that (1) delivers modular AI literacy curriculum adaptable to community context; (2) embeds community governance mechanisms that allow local advisory councils to shape curriculum; (3) provides AI-powered career navigation agents (Mae, Nova, Zuri) that personalize learner pathways; and (4) automates employer-learner matching through an apprenticeship API. Unlike existing EdTech, EquitAI is designed to be funded through Community Benefit Agreements — creating a scalable, sustainable model that can be replicated in any geography with major data center development.

## SBIR Tips

1. **R&D framing is critical** — SBIR funds research and development, not service delivery. Frame EquitAI's work as "developing and testing a novel AI literacy platform" not "running an AI training program."
2. **Commercial potential** — NSF reviewers care deeply about commercialization path. Be specific about CBA market size and scalability.
3. **Innovation** — The CBA integration model IS genuinely novel. Lead with that.
4. **Phase I is proof of concept** — Don't oversell; show that you can validate the approach.
5. **Team qualifications** — SBIR wants to see technical credibility. List any relevant degrees, prior projects, publications.
