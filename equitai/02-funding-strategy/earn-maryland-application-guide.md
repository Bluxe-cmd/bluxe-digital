# EARN Maryland Application Guide

**Program:** Employment Advancement Right Now (EARN Maryland)  
**Administered by:** Maryland Department of Labor  
**Amount:** $100,000–$500,000+ per industry partnership  
**Priority:** 🔴 This is EquitAI's best near-term government funding path  
**Website:** labor.maryland.gov/earn

---

## Why EARN Maryland Is EquitAI's Best Government Opportunity

EARN Maryland funds **industry sector partnerships** — coalitions of employers and training providers working together to train workers for in-demand jobs. This is exactly what EquitAI does.

Unlike most government grants that require you to prove you've already done it, EARN Maryland is designed to **fund organizations to build the partnership**. You come in with employers committed, a training model ready, and a workforce need documented — and EARN Maryland provides the capital.

**What makes this different from other government programs:**
- Relatively fast decision timeline (3–6 months vs. 12+ for federal)
- Maryland-specific — EquitAI's Maryland focus is a feature, not a limitation
- Employer co-investment is valued but not required at full match
- Technology and AI sector qualifies
- Re-entry populations are specifically valued

## EARN Maryland Program Requirements (Verify at labor.maryland.gov/earn)

### Eligibility
- **Lead Applicant:** Must be a nonprofit, trade association, employer group, or workforce intermediary
- **Partners:** Minimum 2–3 employers willing to hire program graduates
- **Training Provider:** Can be the lead applicant or a partner (EquitAI can be either)
- **Target Population:** Priority given to: unemployed, underemployed, people with barriers to employment, re-entry populations
- **Industry:** Must be a growing industry with documented workforce need in Maryland

### EquitAI EARN Maryland Strategy

**Option A: EquitAI as Lead Applicant (Preferred)**
- EquitAI forms an "AI Workforce Partnership" with 3–4 employer partners
- EquitAI applies as lead, commits to training X participants
- Employers commit to interviewing and hiring graduates
- Funding flows to EquitAI

**Option B: EquitAI as Training Partner**
- A trade association or employer group applies as lead
- EquitAI is named as the training provider
- Funding partially flows to EquitAI for training delivery
- Faster to execute if a lead applicant partner exists

**Recommended path:** Start with Option A. If you can't form the coalition fast enough, explore Option B with a community college (PGCC or CSM) as lead.
