## What is TEDCO Pre-Seed?

TEDCO (Maryland Technology Development Corporation) provides pre-seed funding to early-stage tech companies in Maryland. The Pre-Seed Fund is designed for companies that have a technology concept and need funding to validate the concept and develop early customers.

**Website:** tedcomd.com  
**Email:** preseed@tedcomd.com  
**Phone:** 410-740-9442

---

## Eligibility Requirements (Verify at tedcomd.com)

- [ ] Maryland-based company (registered in Maryland)
- [ ] Technology-driven business model (AI/software qualifies)
- [ ] Early stage — no more than $1M in prior investment
- [ ] Founder(s) primarily own the company (no institutional majority)
- [ ] Not a service company without a technology product component
- [ ] Full-time commitment from at least one founder

**EquitAI eligibility notes:**
- ✅ Maryland-based
- ✅ AI/software platform = technology-driven
- ✅ Likely early stage
- ⚠️ Ensure technology product is clearly defined (the AI literacy OS platform, agent system)
- ⚠️ Confirm founder full-time commitment status

## Application Components

TEDCO Pre-Seed typically requires:

### 1. Executive Summary (1–2 pages)
**What to include:**
- Problem statement (AI workforce gap in Maryland)
- Solution (EquitAI OS platform)
- Market size (Maryland AI workforce demand)
- Business model (CBA contracts + employer fees + government contracts)
- Team (Bianca + any co-founders)
- Ask ($50,000 pre-seed)
- Use of funds (see below)

**Template — Use this framework:**

> **Problem:** Maryland's data center economy is expanding rapidly, but communities adjacent to data centers — particularly in Prince George's and Charles Counties — lack AI workforce skills to access the economic opportunity being created. Over [X] data center projects are approved or under construction in Maryland, representing [X] billion in investment. Yet fewer than 10% of residents within 10 miles of these facilities have AI-related credentials.
>
> **Solution:** EquitAI is an AI literacy operating system — modular, community-governed, and designed for workforce outcomes. Our platform delivers stackable AI credentials to high school students and adult learners, connected directly to employer apprenticeship pipelines and data center community benefit agreements.
>
> **Market:** Maryland workforce development market is $[X]B annually. AI-specific training is growing 40%+ YoY. Data center CBAs create $50M+ in annual training obligations in Maryland alone.
>
> **Business Model:** Revenue from CBA training contracts ($120K Year 1), employer training fees ($80K), and government workforce contracts ($100K).
>
> **Team:** Bianca [Last Name], CEO — [2-sentence bio]. [Other team members]
>
> **Ask:** $50,000 to fund [specific deliverables — see use of funds below]

### 3. Technology Description
**What TEDCO wants to know:**
- What is the technology (not just the program)?
- How is it proprietary or defensible?
- What is the development stage?

**EquitAI technology narrative:**
> EquitAI's technology platform includes: (1) An adaptive learning management system built on [platform/stack] that delivers modular AI literacy curriculum with community customization capability; (2) Named AI agent tools (Mae, Nova, Zuri, Atlas, Reed) that personalize learning pathways and connect learners to employment opportunities; (3) A community governance dashboard that allows local advisory councils to review curriculum content, learning outcomes, and data usage in real time; (4) An employer API that matches EquitAI graduates to employer apprenticeship openings.

**Key data points to include:**
- US AI workforce training market size: $8.4B (2024), growing 38% CAGR
- Maryland data center investment: $4B+ in approved/under-construction projects (2023–2026)
- Maryland workforce development contracts: $[X]M annually through MDOL
- AI job postings in Maryland: [X]% increase 2022–2024 (use BLS or LinkedIn data)

### 5. Traction Evidence (Critical)
**Even pre-revenue, show:**
- Letters of interest from employers or school partners
- Any MOU or partnership agreements signed
- Pilot commitments or waitlist (any community members interested)
- Any prior grants, awards, or recognition
- Relevant partnerships with community colleges, libraries, workforce centers

**Action:** Before submitting, get at least 2–3 letters of interest from potential partners.

## Tips for TEDCO Reviewers

Based on TEDCO's historical funding patterns:
1. **Technology specificity matters** — Don't describe a "program," describe a "platform"
2. **Maryland focus is critical** — Emphasize Maryland market, Maryland partners, Maryland impact
3. **Founder commitment** — TEDCO wants to know you're all-in
4. **Early validation beats projections** — 1 real LOI > 100 projected customers
5. **Exit or growth path** — Even at pre-seed, mention potential for scale (employer licensing, national expansion of CBA model)
