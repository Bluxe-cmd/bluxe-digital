## Abell Foundation

**Website:** abell.org  
**Focus:** Baltimore and Maryland economic opportunity, workforce development, equity  
**Amount Range:** $10,000–$100,000 (typical first grant)  
**Deadline:** Rolling (LOIs accepted year-round)  
**Contact:** 410-547-1300 | info@abell.org

### Abell Fit Assessment
EquitAI fits Abell's priorities:
- ✅ Baltimore/Maryland focus
- ✅ Economic opportunity (workforce development)
- ✅ Equity and community benefit
- ✅ Technology for social good
- ✅ Innovative model (CBA-integrated)

### Abell LOI Template

> **To:** Abell Foundation Program Staff  
> **Re:** Letter of Inquiry — EquitAI AI Literacy Workforce Pathway  
> **Date:** [Date]

> Dear Abell Foundation Team,

> I am writing on behalf of EquitAI to request a meeting and submit this letter of inquiry for a $[25,000–50,000] grant to launch Maryland's first CBA-integrated AI literacy workforce pathway for high school students and adult learners in Prince George's and Charles Counties.

> **The Problem**  
> Maryland's data center economy is growing rapidly, with over $4 billion in investment since 2020. Yet the communities closest to these facilities — predominantly Black and brown households earning below the state median — are not benefiting. The disconnect is not a pipeline problem; it's an access and infrastructure problem. There is no community-rooted system to translate AI workforce demand into accessible, outcomes-focused training for residents who need it most.

> **EquitAI's Solution**  
> EquitAI is an AI literacy operating system that delivers stackable credentials to high school seniors and adult learners through community-based facilitators, employer-connected apprenticeship pipelines, and community-governed curriculum. Our model is designed to be sustained beyond grant funding through data center Community Benefit Agreements — a structural funding mechanism that ties data center development to community training investment.

> **What We're Asking For**  
> $[Amount] over [timeframe] to: launch our first two cohorts (50 total learners); certify 4 community facilitators; establish 3 employer partnerships with apprenticeship commitments; and build the evidence base needed to secure CBA funding from active Maryland data center projects.

> **Why Now**  
> Three major data center projects in Prince George's and Charles Counties are currently in community review processes that include CBA negotiations. EquitAI is positioned to be the workforce training partner named in these agreements — but we need seed funding to demonstrate capability before those negotiations close.

> I would welcome a 30-minute conversation to discuss fit and next steps.

> Sincerely,  
> Bianca [Last Name]  
> Founder, EquitAI  
> [phone] | [email]

## Annie E. Casey Foundation

**Website:** aecf.org  
**Focus:** Children, youth, and families — especially economic mobility pathways  
**Amount Range:** $25,000–$100,000+  
**Deadline:** Invitation-based (build relationship first)  
**Contact:** Build relationship through Casey's Maryland-based staff

### Casey Fit Assessment
- ✅ Youth pathway focus (high school students)
- ✅ Economic mobility
- ✅ Community-based, place-based model
- ✅ Maryland connection (Casey HQ is in Baltimore)
- ✅ Systems change approach
- ⚠️ Casey typically requires a relationship before funding — attend their events, get introduced

### Casey Strategy
1. Attend an Annie E. Casey event in Maryland
2. Connect with their Baltimore program staff on LinkedIn
3. Share EquitAI's framework and ask for a 30-minute informational meeting
4. Do NOT cold-apply — build the relationship first
