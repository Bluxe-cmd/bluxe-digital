# VLT Community Grant Application Guide

**Program:** Maryland Lottery — Video Lottery Terminal (VLT) Community Impact Grants  
**Amount:** $5,000–$25,000 (typical range)  
**Requirements:** Maryland nonprofit or fiscally sponsored organization  
**Priority:** 🟡 Research current round dates at mdlottery.com

---

## Overview

Maryland's VLT program directs a percentage of gaming revenue to community benefit grants. The program has multiple categories, and EquitAI's workforce development focus can qualify under:
- **Education and Workforce Development** grants
- **Community Economic Development** grants

**Note:** Requirements may vary by year and grant round. Always verify current requirements at [mdlottery.com](https://mdlottery.com).

---

## VLT Grant Program Types

### Community Initiative Fund
- Focus: Community development, economic development, workforce
- Amount: Up to $25,000
- Nonprofit status: Required (501(c)(3) or fiscal sponsor)

### Education Excellence Award
- Focus: Education programs with clear outcomes
- Amount: $5,000–$15,000
- For: Organizations serving underserved students

### Workforce Development Awards
- Focus: Job training, career readiness, skills development
- Amount: $10,000–$25,000
- For: Organizations with measurable workforce outcomes

## Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Maryland-based organization
- [ ] 501(c)(3) nonprofit status OR fiscal sponsorship arrangement
- [ ] Program serves Maryland residents
- [ ] Clear program outcomes and metrics
- [ ] Geographic focus includes VLT host communities (Baltimore, PG County, Anne Arundel, Cecil County)
- [ ] No current state debarment
- [ ] Financial statements available (if established organization)

**EquitAI VLT Strategy:**
- If EquitAI is not yet a 501(c)(3), identify a **fiscal sponsor** (see list below)
- Apply for Workforce Development category
- Emphasize PG County and Charles County impact (VLT host communities)

## Fiscal Sponsor Options (If EquitAI Lacks 501(c)(3))

A fiscal sponsor is an existing nonprofit that accepts grant funds on EquitAI's behalf, allowing EquitAI to apply for foundation and government grants that require nonprofit status.

**Potential Maryland Fiscal Sponsors:**

| Organization | Focus | Contact |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| Community Foundation of the Chesapeake | Broad community impact | cfchesapeake.org |
| Baltimore Community Foundation | Baltimore/Maryland equity | bcf.org |
| Network for Good | National fiscal sponsor | networkforgood.org |
| Tides Foundation | Social justice, equity | tides.org |

**How to Approach a Fiscal Sponsor:**
1. Call or email the foundation program officer
2. Explain EquitAI's mission and the grant you're pursuing
3. Ask if they offer fiscal sponsorship services
4. Expect a 5–10% administrative fee on grants received

## Application Narrative Template

**Program Description (500–750 words) — adapt this:**

> **Organization:** EquitAI [or Fiscal Sponsor] on behalf of EquitAI  
> **Program:** AI Literacy Workforce Pathways for Underserved Maryland Communities  
> **Grant Category:** Workforce Development  
> **Amount Requested:** $25,000

> **Problem Statement:**  
> Prince George's County and Charles County are experiencing significant economic expansion from data center development, with over $[X] billion in investment in recent years. Despite this growth, residents of these communities — many of whom are people of color with household incomes below the state median — lack access to the AI-related skills needed to participate in this new economy. Only [X]% of residents in these communities hold any technology credential, and the gap between AI job openings and qualified local applicants is growing.

> **Proposed Solution:**  
> EquitAI's AI Literacy Workforce Pathway provides a 120-hour, stackable credential program for high school students (16–18) and adults (25–45) in Prince George's and Charles Counties. The program includes: curriculum aligned with in-demand AI workforce skills; trained community facilitators who deliver sessions at local partner sites; direct connection to employer apprenticeship opportunities upon completion; and community governance over curriculum content and program priorities.

> **Activities:**  
> With $25,000, EquitAI will: (1) Enroll and serve [X] participants in a 12-week AI literacy cohort at [Partner Site]; (2) Train and certify [X] community facilitators; (3) Partner with [X] local employers to provide apprenticeship interviews to graduates; (4) Deliver [X] hours of instruction across [X] sessions.

> **Outcomes:**  
> By the end of the grant period: [X] participants will complete the 120-hour AI Literacy credential; [X]% of completers will secure employment or educational advancement in AI-related fields; [X] community facilitators will be certified and active; [X] employer partnerships established.

> **Sustainability:**  
> Beyond this grant period, EquitAI's program is funded through: data center Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs) requiring training investments; employer training fees; and government workforce development contracts with MDOL and MSDE. This grant will help EquitAI establish the evidence base needed to secure these structural revenue sources.

## Evaluation Metrics for VLT Report

VLT grants require outcome reporting. Use these metrics:
1. Number of participants enrolled
2. Number completing the program
3. Number gaining employment or advancing to education
4. Average wage gain (pre/post)
5. Participant satisfaction score
6. Employer satisfaction score
7. Number of facilitators trained
