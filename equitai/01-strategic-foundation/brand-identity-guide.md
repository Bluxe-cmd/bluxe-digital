# EquitAI Brand Identity Guide

**Version:** 1.0 | March 2026 | Internal Use

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**Mission:** Equitable AI participation for every community.  
**Vision:** A Maryland where communities most impacted by AI are the ones who understand, shape, and benefit from it.  
**Values:** Community ownership · Radical transparency · Workforce dignity · Place-based design · Intergenerational impact

**Brand Voice:**
- **Tone:** Grounded, direct, community-rooted — not corporate, not academic
- **Language:** Plain English. Avoid jargon. If you must use a technical term, define it.
- **Perspective:** "We" = EquitAI + the communities we serve. Not "we serve them."
- **What we avoid:** Savior language, deficit framing, tech-bro optimism, poverty porn

## 2. Color Palette

| Color Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-----------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Mahogany** | `#4A1C1C` | 74, 28, 28 | Primary brand color; headers, key CTAs |
| **Deep Sage** | `#3D5A47` | 61, 90, 71 | Secondary; community/nature grounding |
| **Gold** | `#C89A2A` | 200, 154, 42 | Accent; awards, highlights, success states |
| **Rose** | `#C46B6B` | 196, 107, 107 | Human/warm accent; community stories |
| **Espresso** | `#2C1A0E` | 44, 26, 14 | Dark text, strong hierarchy |
| **Cream** | `#F5EFE6` | 245, 239, 230 | Background, cards, breathing space |
| **Slate** | `#4A5568` | 74, 85, 104 | Body text, secondary information |

**Color Rules:**
- Never use Mahogany on dark backgrounds
- Gold is used sparingly — only for earned recognition, key stats, key CTAs
- Cream is the primary background (never pure white)
- Ensure 4.5:1 contrast ratio for all text (WCAG AA compliance)

## 3. Typography

| Font | Usage | Style |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Cormorant Garamond** | Display headlines | Serif, dignified, community-rooted |
| **Syne** | Subheadings, labels | Sans-serif, modern, mission-driven |
| **DM Mono** | Data, code, stats | Monospace, trustworthy, precise |
| **Italiana** | Pull quotes, feature text | Elegant serif, voice and humanity |

**Hierarchy:**
- H1: Cormorant Garamond, 48–64px, Espresso
- H2: Cormorant Garamond, 32–40px, Mahogany
- H3: Syne, 20–24px, Deep Sage
- Body: Syne or system sans, 16–18px, Slate
- Data/Stats: DM Mono, 14–16px, Espresso
- Pull Quotes: Italiana, 20–24px, Mahogany italic

## 4. Logo Concept

**Primary Mark:** Interconnected nodes forming a human silhouette — representing community networks and AI simultaneously.

**Logo Elements:**
- Node cluster: 5–7 circles in Mahogany and Deep Sage, connected by thin lines
- Logotype: "EquitAI" in Cormorant Garamond, bold
- Tagline (optional): "Community-Powered AI Literacy"

**Logo Variations:**
- Full horizontal (preferred for headers)
- Stacked vertical (preferred for social avatars)
- Icon-only mark (for favicons, small applications)
- One-color version (Mahogany on Cream, or Cream on Mahogany)

**Logo Don'ts:**
- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not use on busy photographic backgrounds without overlay
- Do not change colors outside the brand palette
- Do not add drop shadows or effects

## 5. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Community Ownership
> "EquitAI is built by and for the communities it serves — not delivered to them."

**Use for:** Foundation pitches, community partner outreach, social media

### Pillar 2: Workforce Dignity
> "AI literacy isn't just about understanding technology — it's about being able to earn from it."

**Use for:** Employer partnerships, TEDCO, VLT, government contracts

### Pillar 3: Place-Based Design
> "We start in Maryland because Maryland's communities deserve Maryland-specific solutions."

**Use for:** Maryland-specific funders, GSF tax credits, state government outreach

### Pillar 4: Radical Transparency
> "Every data point, every decision, every curriculum update — communities can see it."

**Use for:** Trust-based philanthropy, community governance pitches, edtech differentiation

### Pillar 5: Intergenerational Impact
> "High school students and returning workers, learning together, building the future together."

**Use for:** High school pathway programs, workforce training, employer pipeline stories

## 6. Stakeholder-Specific Messaging

### For High School Students
**Lead with:** "Get paid to learn AI before you graduate."  
**Emphasize:** Stackable credentials, employer pipelines, peer cohort learning, real projects  
**Avoid:** Academic jargon, "AI is the future" platitudes

### For Adult Learners / Career Changers
**Lead with:** "Your experience matters. AI skills will make it pay more."  
**Emphasize:** Career outcomes, wage data, flexible schedule, community support  
**Avoid:** Starting-from-scratch framing, youth-oriented language

### For Employers
**Lead with:** "Your best future hires are in EquitAI right now."  
**Emphasize:** Pre-screened, workforce-ready candidates; training tax credits; CBA compliance  
**Avoid:** Charity framing, DEI-only language without ROI

### For Government Funders (MDOL, MSDE, MEC)
**Lead with:** "Maryland's AI workforce gap is a policy risk. EquitAI is the solution."  
**Emphasize:** Employment metrics, community college alignment, sector demand, Maryland-specific data  
**Avoid:** Academic language, vague "awareness" outcomes

### For Data Center Developers
**Lead with:** "Your CBA obligation and your talent pipeline — solved in one partnership."  
**Emphasize:** CBA compliance, training deliverables, community goodwill, employment numbers  
**Avoid:** Confrontational language (even in CBA negotiations, lead with partnership)

### For Foundation Funders
**Lead with:** "Systems change, not charity — EquitAI funds itself after Year 2."  
**Emphasize:** Structural funding model (CBAs), community governance, policy outcomes  
**Avoid:** Dependency on continued grants, vague community benefit claims

## 8. Photography and Visual Style

**Style:** Documentary-realistic. Real people, real spaces, real community.  
**Never use:** Stock photos of generic people, AI robot imagery, "tech bro" aesthetics  
**Preferred:** Photos of facilitators teaching, students working on projects, community members in discussion  
**Color treatment:** Warm tones, slight film-grain filter, Mahogany/Gold overlays sparingly  
**Representation:** Majority people of color, mixed gender, mixed age (HS through adult)
