# EquitAI OS Architecture Brief

**Classification:** Internal Strategic | Version 1.0 | March 2026

---

## 1. What EquitAI Is (The One-Sentence Version)

EquitAI is an **AI literacy operating system** that embeds equitable AI education and workforce pathways directly into communities most impacted by the AI economy — through distributed curriculum, community governance, and employer pipelines.

---

## 2. The Operating System Metaphor (Why It Matters)

Unlike a "program" or "course" (which are finite, vendor-controlled, and replaceable), an **operating system**:

- **Runs underneath everything else** — it's infrastructure, not a product
- **Is community-owned and governed** — communities customize and control it
- **Is distributed** — not dependent on one school, one nonprofit, one funder
- **Integrates multiple layers** — curriculum, workforce, policy, governance
- **Gets stronger over time** — network effects, data, partner integrations

**When we say "OS," we mean:**
> "EquitAI is the infrastructure layer for equitable AI participation in your community."

This framing is critical for:
- Government contracts (infrastructure = recurring, not one-time)
- Employer partnerships (pipeline = systemic, not ad-hoc training)
- CBA negotiations (systemic benefit = community benefit obligation)
- Foundation funding (systems change = higher-impact grants)

---

## 3. Architecture Layers

### Layer 1: Curriculum Engine
**What it is:** Modular, stackable AI literacy curriculum for HS students and adult learners.

**Components:**
- Track A — AI Foundations (40 hours, HS/adult entry level)
- Track B — Applied AI for the Workforce (80 hours, stackable credential)
- Track C — AI Policy and Community Impact (20 hours, civic track)
- Track D — AI Technical Pathways (120 hours, near-hire ready)

**Delivery:**
- In-person at partner sites (community colleges, libraries, workforce centers)
- Hybrid (self-paced modules + cohort sessions)
- Employer-embedded (on-site at data centers, tech firms)

**Credential Output:**
- EquitAI Certificate of Completion (all tracks)
- MHEC-aligned college credit (Track B + D, pending)
- NCCER/CompTIA alignment (Track D, workforce credentialing)

---

### Layer 2: Facilitator Network
**What it is:** Trained local facilitators who deliver curriculum and anchor community trust.

**Who they are:**
- Community educators (teachers, workforce trainers)
- Returning citizens with tech interest/background
- Recent graduates of EquitAI Track D
- Library staff and nonprofit program coordinators

**Facilitator Model:**
- 40-hour facilitator certification
- Paid delivery ($35–$55/hour, depending on contract)
- Ongoing PD through monthly cohort calls
- Represented in curriculum governance

---

### Layer 3: Community Governance
**What it is:** Mechanism for communities to shape what EquitAI teaches and how it evolves.

**Structure:**
- **Local Advisory Councils** — one per partner site (5–8 members: parents, employers, students, facilitators)
- **Regional Policy Board** — quarterly, feeds into curriculum updates
- **Data Governance Panel** — community control over student learning data

**Why This Matters for Funding:**
- Government funders require community input metrics
- CBAs typically require community representation
- GSF tax credit organizations may require advisory governance
- Distinguishes EquitAI from extractive edtech (key for trust fundraising)

---

### Layer 4: Employer Pipeline
**What it is:** Direct connection from EquitAI graduates to paid employment.

**Employer Commitments (per partnership agreement):**
- Minimum 10 apprenticeship slots/year (paid, $18–$25/hr)
- Interview guarantee for Track D completers
- Curriculum input on in-demand skills
- Co-funding option (workforce training tax credits)

**Pipeline Metrics:**
- 6-month employment rate (target: 70%+ in AI/tech adjacent roles)
- Average wage increase from pre-training (target: $8/hr+)
- Employer satisfaction score (target: 85%+)

---

### Layer 5: Policy Integration
**What it is:** Positioning EquitAI graduates and communities as AI policy participants, not just consumers.

**Components:**
- Student policy research projects (Track C)
- Facilitator representation at county planning meetings
- Data center CBA advocacy toolkit
- State legislative briefing support
