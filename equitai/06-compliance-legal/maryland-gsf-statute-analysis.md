## Overview: Maryland Workforce Training Tax Credit

Maryland offers several tax credit mechanisms for employers who invest in workforce training. The most relevant for EquitAI is the **Maryland Employer Security Clearance Costs Tax Credit** and the broader **Qualified Training Expense Tax Credits** administered through the Maryland Department of Commerce.

The "GSF" reference in EquitAI's strategy refers to both:
1. **Governor's Strategic Fund** — discretionary economic development funding
2. **Qualified workforce training tax credits** — employer credits for certified training expenditures
3. **The Goldstein-Schiff Foundation** — private philanthropy (separate from state programs)

All three can be pursued simultaneously with different strategies.

## Program 1: Maryland Qualified Job Training Tax Credit

**Authority:** Maryland Tax-General Article §10-725 (verify current version)  
**Administered by:** Maryland Department of Commerce  

### What It Does
Allows employers to claim a tax credit equal to a percentage of qualified training expenses for Maryland employees in qualifying industries.

### Qualifying Industries (Verify with Commerce)
- Information technology / AI / data science
- Advanced manufacturing
- Cybersecurity
- Life sciences
- Aerospace and defense
- Clean energy

**EquitAI's positioning:** EquitAI trains workers for AI/data science roles → qualifies as information technology workforce training.

### Qualification Criteria (General Framework — Verify Current Requirements)
- [ ] Training program must be certified by Maryland Department of Commerce
- [ ] Training must be for Maryland employees at Maryland locations
- [ ] Training must be in a qualifying industry sector
- [ ] Employer must be a Maryland-registered business entity
- [ ] Training provider (EquitAI) must meet certification standards

### Tax Credit Structure (Approximate — Verify)
- Credit rate: Typically 50–100% of qualified training expenses
- Per-employee cap: Verify current cap
- Aggregate employer cap: Verify current cap
- Carry-forward: Typically 5–10 years

## Program 2: EARN Maryland (Industry Partnerships)

**Authority:** EARN Maryland Act (MD Labor & Employment §11-701 et seq.)  
**Administered by:** Maryland Department of Labor  
**Website:** labor.maryland.gov/earn

### What EARN Maryland Does
EARN Maryland (Employment Advancement Right Now) funds **industry-sector partnerships** — coalitions of employers and training providers who collaboratively train workers for in-demand jobs.

**This is EquitAI's best near-term fit for government-funded training.**

### Structure
- **Lead applicant:** An industry association, employer group, or workforce organization
- **Partners:** Employers who will hire graduates + training provider (EquitAI) + community partners
- **Funding:** State grants to the partnership, paid per-trainee or per-program
- **Amount:** Grants range from $100,000–$500,000+ per partnership

### EquitAI EARN Maryland Strategy
1. Identify 3–5 Maryland employers in the AI/tech sector willing to co-apply
2. Form a sector partnership: "Maryland AI Workforce Partnership"
3. Apply to EARN Maryland as the training provider (EquitAI) within the partnership
4. Employers commit to hiring graduates; EquitAI commits to training them

**Key contacts for EARN Maryland:**
- Maryland Department of Labor Workforce Development: labor.maryland.gov/earn
- EARN Maryland Program Manager: 410-767-2170

## Program 3: Governor's Strategic Fund (Discretionary)

**Authority:** MD Economic Development Article §5-1401  
**Administered by:** Maryland Department of Commerce  

### What the Governor's Strategic Fund Does
The GSF is a discretionary economic development fund used by the Governor to attract or retain businesses and create jobs in Maryland. It is **not a standard grant program** — it is negotiated case-by-case with companies making significant Maryland investments.

### How EquitAI Could Access GSF
EquitAI is unlikely to directly receive GSF as a standalone training organization. However:
1. **Data center developer negotiations:** When negotiating with a data center developer, ask their government affairs team whether they are receiving or negotiating GSF incentives — and make EquitAI's training program part of their incentive package commitments
2. **Job creation pathway:** If EquitAI creates 25+ Maryland jobs, the company itself could qualify for GSF business retention support
3. **Legislative champion:** Work with PG County delegation to include EquitAI-type training obligations in any GSF agreement with a data center developer

### GSF Contact
Maryland Department of Commerce — Business Development Division  
410-767-6300 | commerce.maryland.gov

## Program 4: Maryland Apprenticeship and Training Program (MATP)

**Administered by:** Maryland Department of Labor  
**Website:** labor.maryland.gov/labor/appr  

### What MATP Does
MATP supports the development and expansion of apprenticeship programs in Maryland. Employers who register apprenticeship programs get access to:
- Wage subsidies for apprentices
- Tax credits for apprenticeship training costs
- Technical assistance in program development

### EquitAI MATP Strategy
1. Register EquitAI Track D completers as apprenticeship candidates
2. Work with employer partners to register as Registered Apprenticeship programs
3. Access MATP wage subsidies to reduce employer cost of hiring EquitAI graduates
4. Use MATP as a talking point in employer outreach ("hiring through us comes with wage subsidies")

**Contact:** Maryland Apprenticeship and Training Program — 410-767-2246

## GSF Tax Credit Employer Sponsor — How to Find One

To access Maryland's workforce training tax credits, EquitAI needs an **employer sponsor** — a Maryland company that:
1. Has Maryland tax liability (to use credits)
2. Wants to hire AI-skilled workers
3. Is willing to pay EquitAI for training their future/current employees
4. Can certify the training expenditures on their tax return
