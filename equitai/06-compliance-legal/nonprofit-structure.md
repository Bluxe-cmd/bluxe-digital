# EquitAI Nonprofit Structure Guide

**Decision Needed:** Choose and execute legal structure by Month 2  
**Recommendation:** Hybrid — LLC (operating) + 501(c)(3) (fundraising)  
**Legal Advisor Required:** Consult a Maryland attorney before finalizing

---

## The Structural Decision

EquitAI's funding mix requires a **dual-entity strategy**:

| Entity | Type | Purpose | Required For |
|--------|------|---------|-------------|
| EquitAI, LLC | For-profit LLC | CBA contracts, employer fees, operations | Flexibility, speed, contracts |
| EquitAI Foundation (or EquitAI Education Fund) | 501(c)(3) | Grants, VLT, foundations, tax-deductible donations | VLT, foundations, GSF tax credit |

**Why both?**
- CBA contracts and employer training fees are business income — better in an LLC
- VLT grants, foundation grants, and tax-deductible donations require nonprofit status
- The LLC can contract with the nonprofit for services (appropriate at arm's length)
- Maryland allows this structure; consult an attorney for proper governance

---

## Option 1: LLC Only (Short-Term, If Fiscal Sponsor Used)

**How it works:**
- EquitAI operates as an LLC
- For grants requiring nonprofit status, use a **fiscal sponsor** (existing 501(c)(3) that holds the grant and subcontracts to EquitAI)
- Faster to set up (LLC in Maryland = 1–2 weeks)
- Less administrative overhead

**Pros:**
- Fast (can be operational in 2 weeks)
- Flexible contracts and operations
- No IRS nonprofit application delay

**Cons:**
- Fiscal sponsor takes 5–10% fee
- Can't directly receive tax-deductible donations
- Some funders won't use fiscal sponsors

**Best For:** Year 1 while nonprofit application is pending

---

## Option 2: 501(c)(3) Only (Longer-Term)

**How it works:**
- EquitAI operates entirely as a nonprofit
- All revenue streams (including CBA contracts) flow through the nonprofit
- Eligible for all grants, tax-deductible donations, government programs

**Pros:**
- Simplified structure
- Full access to grant funding
- Tax-deductible donations
- GSF tax credit eligibility

**Cons:**
- IRS Form 1023 takes 6–12 months for full approval (expedited 1023-EZ available for smaller orgs)
- Board governance requirements slow some decisions
- Unrelated business income (employer training fees) may have tax implications

**Best For:** Year 2+ as the primary entity once approved

---

## Option 3: Hybrid LLC + 501(c)(3) — RECOMMENDED

**How it works:**
- **EquitAI, LLC**: Holds CBA contracts, employer training contracts, tech platform
- **EquitAI Education Fund, Inc. (501(c)(3))**: Holds grants, donations, VLT, GSF tax credit proceeds
- The two entities contract with each other at arm's length
- Bianca is CEO of both; separate boards/governance for each

**Pros:**
- Best of both worlds — speed (LLC) + grant eligibility (nonprofit)
- Protects LLC from nonprofit governance overhead
- Separates earned revenue from charitable revenue for accounting
- Clear structure for investors and funders

**Cons:**
- Two entities = more administrative cost
- Requires careful arm's-length contracting between entities
- Attorney and accountant oversight required

**Best For:** EquitAI's mixed revenue model long-term

## Step-by-Step: Set Up the LLC First (This Month)

### Step 1: Choose a Name
- "EquitAI, LLC" — verify availability at Maryland SDAT: sdat.dat.maryland.gov
- Check trademark: USPTO.gov
- Check domain: equitai.org / equitai.com

### Step 2: File Articles of Organization (Maryland)
- File with Maryland State Department of Assessments & Taxation (SDAT)
- Online: businessexpress.maryland.gov
- Fee: $100 (verify current fee)
- Timeline: 1–7 business days (online)
- Required information: LLC name, registered agent, principal office

### Step 3: Obtain EIN (Federal Employer Identification Number)
- Apply at irs.gov/ein-online — free, immediate
- Needed for: bank account, contracts, tax filing

### Step 4: Open Business Bank Account
- Recommended: Maryland-based bank (M&T Bank, Sandy Spring Bank, PNC) or online (Relay, Mercury)
- Bring: Articles of organization + EIN + Bianca's ID

### Step 5: Create Operating Agreement
- Governs how the LLC is managed
- Specifies: Bianca's ownership %, other member %s (if any), profit distribution, decision-making
- Have an attorney draft this if there are other members

### Step 6: Register for Maryland Taxes
- Maryland Business Registration: taxes.marylandtaxes.gov
- May need: Sales tax registration (if selling taxable services), employer registration (when you hire)

## Step-by-Step: Apply for 501(c)(3) (Month 2–4)

### Step 1: Incorporate as a Nonprofit Corporation in Maryland
- File Articles of Incorporation with SDAT
- Must include nonprofit purpose language and dissolution clause
- File IRS-compliant language about no private inurement

### Step 2: Adopt Bylaws
- Governs the nonprofit corporation
- Must include: Board structure, officer roles, meeting requirements, conflict of interest policy

### Step 3: Recruit Initial Board of Directors
- Minimum: 3 directors (recommend 5 for credibility)
- Board should NOT be majority Bianca's family members
- Recommended board composition for EquitAI:
  - 1 technology/AI professional
  - 1 workforce development expert
  - 1 community member from PG or Charles County
  - 1 attorney or accountant
  - 1 educator or community college representative
  - Bianca (ex officio, CEO — may or may not vote depending on structure)

### Step 4: Obtain EIN for the Nonprofit
- Same process as LLC — different EIN

### Step 5: File IRS Form 1023 (or 1023-EZ)
- **1023-EZ:** For organizations with projected annual gross receipts ≤ $50,000 (Year 1 might qualify)
  - Faster (2–4 weeks approval) | Fee: $275
  - File at pay.gov
- **Form 1023:** Full application for larger organizations
  - Processing: 6–12 months | Fee: $600
  - Required for most foundation funders who require full 501(c)(3)

**EquitAI Recommendation:** File 1023-EZ initially if projected grants < $50K; be prepared to re-file 1023 in Year 2 as revenue grows.

### Step 6: Apply for Maryland State Tax Exemption
- File with Comptroller of Maryland: 410-260-7980
- Reference federal 1023 approval

### Step 7: Register as a Charitable Organization in Maryland
- Required if soliciting charitable donations in Maryland
- File with Maryland Secretary of State: sos.maryland.gov
- Registration fee and annual renewal required

## Board Recruitment: Who to Ask

### Immediate Ask (Month 1–2)
Start the conversation before the nonprofit is formed.

**Tech + AI professional:**
- Search: LinkedIn for "AI" + "Maryland" + "nonprofit board" or "advisor"
- Ask: TEDCO portfolio companies for board referrals
- Target: Someone at a Maryland data center, tech firm, or federal AI contractor

**Workforce development expert:**
- Ask: PGCC or CSM Workforce Director if they'd serve as community advisor
- Contact: Maryland Workforce Association (mdworkforce.com) for referrals
- Target: Someone who has run government workforce contracts

**Community member:**
- Recruit from: PG County or Charles County community organizations you're already engaging
- Look for: Parent of a HS student, library board member, faith community leader

**Attorney or accountant:**
- Ask your attorney (once retained) if they'd serve as pro bono board member
- Contact: Maryland Volunteer Lawyers for the Arts or Maryland Pro Bono Resource Center for referrals

**Educator:**
- Contact a community college dean or CTE director you're in conversation with
- Target: Someone who can open doors to institutional partnerships

## Annual Compliance Checklist

Once structured, maintain compliance:

**LLC (Annual):**
- [ ] Maryland Annual Report and Personal Property Tax Return (due April 15): sdat.dat.maryland.gov
- [ ] Federal tax return (Form 1065 if multi-member; Schedule C if single member)
- [ ] Maryland business income tax
- [ ] Operating agreement updates (if any changes)

**501(c)(3) Nonprofit (Annual):**
- [ ] IRS Form 990 (or 990-EZ or 990-N depending on revenue) — due 5 months after fiscal year end
- [ ] Maryland Charitable Registration renewal: sos.maryland.gov
- [ ] Maryland nonprofit annual report: sdat.dat.maryland.gov
- [ ] Board meeting minutes documented
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy reviewed annually
- [ ] Financial statements (audit required if > $500K in grants for some funders)
