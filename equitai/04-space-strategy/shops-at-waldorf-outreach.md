# Shops at Waldorf — Space Partnership Strategy

**Location:** Shops at Waldorf, 3200 Crain Highway, Waldorf, MD 20603  
**Priority:** 🔴 Week 1 Outreach  
**Type:** Retail space repurposing for training/workforce center

---

## Why Shops at Waldorf?

The Shops at Waldorf is a partially vacant regional mall in Charles County — one of EquitAI's target geographies. Across the country, malls are repurposing vacant anchor and inline space for education, workforce training, healthcare, and community services. This is a major opportunity for EquitAI to secure low-cost or subsidized space.

**Strategic Logic:**
- Charles County workforce center = serves Waldorf/La Plata community
- Mall space = cheap, accessible, parking, public transit
- Mall management = motivated to fill vacancies with community-anchoring tenants
- Data center CBA obligation = could fund the training done at this space
- Community visibility = community sees EquitAI in their neighborhood

---

## Space Needs

For EquitAI's initial operations:
- **Minimum viable space:** 1,000–1,500 sq ft
- **Ideal space:** 2,000–3,000 sq ft
- **Key requirements:**
  - Wi-Fi/internet capable
  - Classroom-style setup (20–30 seats)
  - Accessible by public transit
  - ADA compliant
  - Hours: 8am–9pm (evening cohorts)
- **Rent target:** $5–$12/sq ft annually (below market, community use terms)

## Negotiation Points for Mall Space

When negotiating with mall management:

1. **Community anchor framing:** Position EquitAI as a community anchor that drives foot traffic — not a tenant that just pays rent
2. **Government backing:** Mention potential MDOL/MSDE contracts that fund the program (reduces risk perception)
3. **Social impact value:** Mall can use EquitAI's presence for press, community relations, tax incentives
4. **CBA framing:** "We are funded in part by data center community benefit agreements" = strong financial backing story
5. **Short-term pilot ask:** Start with 3–6 month pilot at minimal cost; prove value; then negotiate full lease
