# EquitAI Property & Space Strategy

**Overview:** How to secure training space in target geographies  
**Priority:** 🟡 Month 1–2  
**Geographies:** Prince George's County | Charles County | Southern Maryland

---

## Space Strategy Principles

1. **Start in community, not in office buildings** — credibility comes from being where people are
2. **Leverage existing infrastructure first** — libraries, community colleges, faith spaces are free or cheap
3. **Use space as a community investment argument** — "We bring 50+ community members to your space each month"
4. **Match space to cohort schedule** — evening cohorts need evening access; negotiate this upfront
5. **Treat space partners as program partners** — not just landlords

---

## Tier 1: Free or Near-Free Space (Start Here)

### Public Libraries
**Why:** Free, trusted, accessible, ADA compliant, community-neutral space

| Library System | Branches | Contact |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| PG County Memorial Library System | 19 branches | pgcmls.info |
| Charles County Public Library | 4 branches | ccplonline.info |
| St. Mary's County Library | 4 branches | 301-475-2846 |

**How to Access:**
1. Call the branch manager (not the main number — branch managers have more discretion)
2. Ask about "community program space" for a workforce development series
3. Most libraries have meeting rooms bookable monthly for regular programs
4. Some have dedicated "makerspace" or learning lab areas suitable for tech training
5. Key ask: Multi-month booking (not one-off) at the same time/day weekly

**Space specs available at libraries:**
- Computer labs: 10–20 stations (most large branches)
- Meeting rooms: 15–40 seats
- Hours: Typically 9am–9pm weekdays, 9am–5pm weekends

**Pitch to Library Manager:**
> "We're an AI workforce training organization and we'd like to run a free program for community members at your branch. We'll bring 15–20 residents each week for 10 weeks. No cost to the library. We just need the meeting room and ideally your computer lab. Can we discuss?"

---

### Faith Community Spaces
**Why:** High community trust, often underutilized weekday/evening space, diversity of locations

**How to Access:**
1. Connect with the faith community liaison in your network
2. Ask to present at one Sunday service or midweek meeting
3. Pitch: "We'd like to use your fellowship hall on [evenings] for a free workforce training program for community members"
4. Offer: Community members who attend = built-in participant recruitment

**Typical terms:** Free or small cleaning fee ($50–$100/month)  
**Key consideration:** Secular program — make clear program is open to all regardless of faith affiliation

---

### Other Vacant Retail / Mall Space
**Why:** High vacancy nationally = strong negotiating leverage  
**Target:** Marlow Heights Shopping Center (PG County) | Iverson Mall | Southern Maryland properties  
**How:** Identify current property management; pitch community anchor tenant value

**Negotiation framework:**
- Ask for rent-free for 3–6 months (pilot period)
- Propose: EquitAI pays utilities only during pilot
- After pilot: Below-market rate ($5–$10/sq ft annually vs. $15–$25/sq ft market)
- Offer: Press release, community recognition, county economic development support

---

## Space Requirements by Cohort Size

| Cohort Size | Min Sq Ft | Computer Stations Needed | AV Needed |
|------------|-----------|--------------------------|-----------|
| 10 participants | 400 sq ft | 10 | Projector/screen |
| 20 participants | 700 sq ft | 20 | Projector/screen + speakers |
| 30 participants | 1,000 sq ft | 20 (can share) | Full AV setup |

**Minimum viable setup for 20-person cohort:**
- Room: 700+ sq ft
- Tables and chairs: 20
- Projector or large monitor: 1
- Wi-Fi: 25 Mbps minimum upload/download
- Whiteboard or flip chart: 1
- Access to computers: Either computer lab or participants bring laptops (provide device stipend if needed)

---

## Technology Access Strategy

Not all participants have personal devices. Solutions:

1. **Partner with library for computer lab access** (free)
2. **Negotiate device loan** from a tech partner (Microsoft TEALS, Google.org, Comcast)
3. **Apply for device grant** — many foundations fund device access for workforce programs
4. **Budget device stipend** into CBA or employer training contract ($100/participant for portable keyboard + access)
5. **MDOL EARN Maryland** sometimes funds equipment for registered training programs

**Maryland Digital Equity Act connection:** Governor Moore's digital equity initiatives include device access — research current Maryland broadband + digital equity programs at ost.maryland.gov.

## Multi-Site Strategy (Year 2)

By Year 2, EquitAI should have:
- 1 permanent or semi-permanent home base site (Shops at Waldorf OR community college)
- 3–4 satellite delivery sites (libraries, partner sites)
- Mobile delivery capability (bring program to employer site for employer-funded cohorts)

This multi-site model allows EquitAI to:
- Serve geographically dispersed populations
- Meet CBA-funded cohorts where they live
- Reduce dependence on any single space partner
