# EquitAI Upskilling & High School Pathway Model

**Strategic Focus:** Workforce outcomes for high school students (16–18) and adult career changers  
**Not serving:** K-8 general AI awareness  
**Why:** Workforce training is where CBAs, employer contracts, and government funding intersect

---

## The Strategic Decision: Why Upskilling, Not K-8

This choice is not accidental — it is the economic engine of the entire EquitAI model.

| Dimension | K-8 AI Literacy | EquitAI Upskilling/HS |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Funder type | Districts, curriculum grants | Employers, CBAs, MDOL, MSDE |
| Contract size | $50–$200K/district | $120K–$500K/CBA |
| Revenue model | Curriculum licensing | Training contracts |
| Outcome metric | "Awareness" | Employment + wage gain |
| Time to revenue | 12–18 months | 3–6 months |
| Competitive landscape | Crowded (Code.org, CS4All) | Sparse — EquitAI has first-mover advantage |
| Employer interest | Low | High — they need this talent |
| GSF tax credit eligibility | No | Yes (workforce training) |
| Community urgency | Diffuse | Immediate (adults need jobs now) |

**Bottom line:** K-8 is a brand play. Upskilling is a revenue play. EquitAI wins by being the workforce solution, not the school program.

## Pathway 1: High School AI Career Track

### Who It Serves
- Juniors and seniors at Title I high schools in PG and Charles Counties
- Students who want a tech career but aren't sure about a 4-year degree
- Students interested in the AI economy but have never had access to it

### Program Design

**Entry Point:** Career Exploration Day (1 session, free, at school or community site)  
- Students learn what AI is, what AI jobs pay, what EquitAI offers
- Students and parents fill out interest forms
- School counselors refer students

**Track 1A: AI Foundations (40 hours | 10 weeks | 4 hrs/week)**
- What is AI? How does it work? Who benefits?
- AI tools in daily life (hands-on: ChatGPT, image generators, AI search)
- AI ethics and community impact
- Career mapping: What AI jobs exist? What do they require?
- Credential: EquitAI AI Foundations Certificate

**Track 1B: Applied AI for the Workforce (80 hours | 10 weeks | 8 hrs/week)**
- Prerequisite: Track 1A completion
- Data literacy fundamentals
- AI prompt engineering and tool use for professional contexts
- Project-based learning: students build an AI-assisted solution to a real community problem
- Resume, LinkedIn, interview prep with AI tools
- Credential: EquitAI Workforce Ready Certificate

**Track 1D: AI Technical Pathways (120 hours | 15 weeks | 8 hrs/week)**
- Prerequisite: Track 1B completion
- Python fundamentals
- Machine learning concepts (no graduate math required)
- AI project development (build and deploy a simple AI tool)
- Portfolio development
- Credential: EquitAI Technical Certificate

### High School Partnership Structure

**EquitAI's Ask from Schools:**
- Permission to operate after-school or weekend program (not during school day)
- Student referrals from counselors
- Space in school building or facilitation of community site
- Communication to parents

**EquitAI's Offer to Schools:**
- Free program for students (employer/CBA funded)
- Employment outcomes for graduates (schools want this for ESSA accountability)
- Employer connections (schools value industry relationships)
- Curriculum aligned with CTE standards

**Dual Enrollment Option (Future):**
- Working with PGCC and CSM to award college credit for Track 1B and 1D completions
- Requires MHEC articulation agreement
- Timeline: Year 2

## Pathway 2: Adult AI Upskilling Track

### Who It Serves
- Adults (25–45) in career transition or seeking wage advancement
- Returning citizens re-entering the workforce
- Workers in declining industries (retail, administrative, logistics) seeking tech pivot
- Current employees whose employers are investing in AI upskilling

### Program Design

**Entry Point:** Free Community Info Session (2 hours, at library or partner site)**  
- Learn what AI jobs pay and what skills they require
- Meet past graduates
- Understand the program schedule and commitment
- Apply on-site

**Track 2A: AI Literacy for Career Changers (40 hours | 5 weeks | 8 hrs/week)**
- AI fundamentals from a worker perspective
- AI tools for productivity in any job
- Understanding AI job titles and career ladders
- Workforce documentation: resume, LinkedIn, portfolio
- Credential: EquitAI Career Readiness Certificate

**Track 2B: AI Specialist Training (80 hours | 10 weeks | 8 hrs/week)**
- Prerequisite: Track 2A or demonstrated equivalency
- AI tool specialization (options: data analysis, content automation, customer experience AI, operations AI)
- Project: Implement an AI solution at a current or target employer
- Employer partner connections
- Credential: EquitAI AI Specialist Certificate

**Track 2D: Technical AI Track for Career Changers (120 hours | 15 weeks)**
- For adults with some prior tech background who want to pivot into AI roles
- Mirrors Track 1D above with adult-relevant context
- Strong employer pipeline (data center, federal contractor, tech firm)
- Credential: EquitAI Technical Certificate

### Adult Learner Support Model

Retention is the biggest challenge in adult workforce programs. EquitAI builds in:
- **Participant stipends:** $100/cohort (defrayed by employer/CBA funding)
- **Flexible scheduling:** Evening and weekend cohorts (6pm–9pm weeknights, 9am–2pm Saturdays)
- **Childcare coordination:** Partner with YMCA or Head Start for childcare referral
- **Transit support:** Program at transit-accessible sites; partner with county transit on awareness
- **Peer cohort model:** Learners form a cohort, not just a class — accountability and community
- **Facilitators from the community:** Participants see themselves in the facilitators

## Pathway 3: Returning Citizen AI Reentry Track

### Who It Serves
- Adults recently released from incarceration in Maryland
- Adults on probation or parole seeking employment
- Partnering with: Maryland Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services (DPSCS), Community law offices, reentry programs

### Why This Pathway?
- Maryland DPSCS has a workforce re-entry mandate and funding stream
- AI workforce training = high-paying jobs = reduced recidivism (policy win)
- EquitAI's community facilitator model makes this culturally appropriate
- VLT grants often prioritize reentry populations
- George W. Mitchell Center for Reentry partnerships possible

### Program Design
- Modified Track 2A (40 hours) with reentry-specific context
- Employer partners pre-screened for "fair chance hiring" commitment
- Case manager coordination with reentry support orgs
- Wage range: $18–$25/hour for graduates placed

## Facilitator Model

### Who Can Be a Facilitator
- Community educators with tech background
- Recent Track D graduates (paid apprenticeship as facilitator)
- Community college instructors moonlighting
- Professionals in AI/tech willing to give back (pro bono + small stipend)

### Facilitator Certification (40 hours)
- EquitAI curriculum overview (10 hrs)
- Adult learning and facilitation techniques (10 hrs)
- Community-centered teaching methods (10 hrs)
- Practicum: co-facilitate 1 session (10 hrs)
- Output: EquitAI Certified Facilitator designation

### Facilitator Compensation
- Year 1: $35–$45/hour (contracted, not salaried)
- Year 2: $45–$55/hour + benefits pathway
- Incentive: Employment outcome bonuses (if 80%+ of cohort employed, facilitator earns bonus)

## Employer Pipeline Model

### How It Works
1. EquitAI signs a **Workforce Pipeline Agreement** with employer
2. Employer commits to: interview every EquitAI graduate; hire minimum X per year; pay minimum $Y/hour
3. EquitAI delivers qualified candidates with credentials
4. Employer provides feedback on curriculum (closed loop)
5. EquitAI reports employment outcomes to funders and CBAs

### Target Employer Types
- Data center operations (facilities technicians, operations analysts)
- Federal contractors (IT support, data analyst roles)
- Hospitals and healthcare systems (AI in healthcare — growing fast)
- Financial services firms (AI in operations)
- State and local government (AI implementation roles — growing with Moore administration)

### Employer Value Proposition
> "EquitAI graduates are pre-screened, credential-verified, and community-rooted. They don't just know AI tools — they know how to apply them in real operational contexts. And hiring them meets your CBA commitment, your DEI goals, and your AI workforce needs simultaneously."
