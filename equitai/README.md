## EquitAI at a Glance

### The Problem We Solve
- Data centers are expanding rapidly in Maryland (PG, Charles, Montgomery counties)
- Communities adjacent to data centers get tax burden but no jobs, training, or economic benefit
- High school students and adult workers have no clear pathway into AI/tech careers
- Existing AI literacy programs focus on K-8 or college; the upskilling gap is massive

### Our Solution
1. **AI Literacy Curriculum** — Workforce-ready, stackable credentials for HS students and adults
2. **Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs)** — Negotiate training obligations from data center developers
3. **Employer Partnerships** — Paid apprenticeships and hire pipelines from CBA-funded training
4. **Government Contracts** — MDOL, MSDE, MEC workforce development contracts
5. **Maryland GSF Tax Credits** — Channel employer tax credits into EquitAI program funding

## Key Decision: Business Model Focus

EquitAI targets **upskilling and high school pathways** (not K-8).

**Why:**
- Faster path to employment outcomes (fundable metrics for government)
- CBAs require adult/workforce training — K-8 doesn't qualify
- Employer partnerships require near-hire-ready participants
- Maryland GSF tax credits require workforce-oriented training
- Higher revenue per participant vs. K-8 curriculum licensing

**Who We Serve:**
- High school juniors/seniors (16–18) — dual enrollment pathways
- Adults in career transition (25–45) — upskilling for AI/data economy
- Returning citizens — workforce re-entry programs
- Community college students — stackable credential partnerships
